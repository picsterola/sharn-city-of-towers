The Great Families

Noble Houses of Sharn

Seven major tiefling noble houses, each tied to an archdevil patron, compete for influence in an intricate web of alliances, feuds, and arranged marriages. Below them, lesser families scramble for relevance.

House Vyrune

The most politically active house. Funds Morgrave University faculty, places Lysander on the Board of Provosts, hosts imperial celebrations. Cousins Syra and Kaelor show public tension. Lord Xandovier courts Lady Zemireh. Their influence is expanding but their internal fractures are visible.

House Hanissa

Brokers of peace and rising influence. Sponsored the Epistemological Conference, produced the archaeologist-adventurer Adina of the Dauntless Agency. Lord Denir Hanissa's wedding to Lady Marith Necato ties two houses together. Described as "up-and-coming."

House Boromar / Boromar Clan

Lady Ilyra Boromar's ill-fated gala at Far Hearth (burned to the ground). The Boromar Clan collected Lianna ir'Clarn's entire Ascension Race winnings to settle her father's debts. Straddles the line between noble respectability and organized crime.

House Necato

Vineyards in Breland producing the post-Gloom "Shadow Red" wine. Sponsored free tickets for The Ratcatcher. Lady Marith Necato's upcoming wedding to Lord Denir Hanissa strengthens inter-house ties.

House Mardikov

Sponsors public banquets with hunger-relieving draughts for the poor. Rumored primary investor behind the new Imperial Skydocks in Skyway. Operates with a mix of public charity and private capital ambition.

House Venator

Represented by Sir Benigno the 14th, "impeccably dressed as one would expect from his house." Present at the PCA&ARI event aboard the Lightning Swift. Fashion-forward, status-conscious.

House Absconditus

Lord Perigrine attended the PCA&ARI event. The house name itself — "Absconditus," hidden — suggests a family that operates in shadows. Minimal public profile, maximum private influence.

Lesser Families

Other Notable Houses

House ir'Tain

Councilor Jiran ir'Tain found guilty of three murders including Lord ir'Vown, executed. All assets seized. Family sentenced to surface work camps. A cautionary tale of overreach.

House ir'Roole

Lord Drevan granted divorce from Lady Nora after attempted mariticide. She fled, was spotted with his cousin Lord Malakai on a private sky-barge. A domestic drama playing out across the city.

The Cecillio Family

Lord Jeramiah died in a duel over gambling debts (Nov 2024). Heir Matteo married Liana, goddaughter of the mysterious "Lord & Lady S." Their only true asset is their title.

House Demell

Duke and Duchess Demell had the priceless Tears of the Angels necklace stolen from their Mithral Tower vault. It was later recovered. Duchess Demell wore it publicly to The Ratcatcher revival.

House Corvoni

A family defined by dueling culture. The feud between Atticus and Nerissa Corvoni was brokered by House Hanissa. Their rose-and-swords crest reflects a house where violence and beauty intertwine.

House ir'Vown

Lord ir'Vown was murdered by Councilor Jiran ir'Tain. The family's arcane-scholar crest hints at deeper connections to Morgrave University and the magical establishment of Sharn.
