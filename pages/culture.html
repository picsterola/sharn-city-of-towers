Culture & Society

Theatre, gossip, food, wine, and the social fabric of an infernal metropolis where scandal is entertainment and entertainment is politics.

The Theatre Scene

Death at the Opera

Closed at the Pinnacle Theatre after a 10-year run. Even artificer-enhanced staging couldn't save it.

Va-Va-Va-Voom!

The All-Gender, All-Species Review at the Pitt House Theatre. Nobles attend masked. Closed after Duchess B--'s duel escalated into a mass melee leaving 10 dead.

The Ratcatcher

Classic mystery revival at the Grand Stage. Drew the city's notable nobles. The Necato family sponsored free day-of-show tickets.

What the Kenku Saw

Mystery farce at the Grand Stage. Missing heiress, stolen statue, identical twins, and a Kenku butler.

The Whispering Court

The Art Temple's most ambitious production. Heavy-handed political allegory with breathtaking illusion-infused staging. "See it, if only to doubt your own eyes."

Gossip as Intelligence

Inundatio's columns aren't just entertainment — they're intelligence reports. Who was seen with whom at the theatre reveals political alliances. Lady Claudia Aemilius and Lord Flavius Mericure in a private box. Lady Zemireh on Lord Xandovier Vyrune's arm. Every sighting is a data point in the political map.

Food, Wine & Commerce

Shadow Red wine from House Necato's post-Gloom vineyards. The Aundairian Easel restaurant in Upper Menthis, owned by Marquis Elar d'Vino. The Three Feasts of Aundair with parades and delicacies. Talenta Plains tribal instruments selling at premium prices after the Silver Jubilee.

Dueling Culture

Duels shape politics as much as votes. Atticus vs. Nerissa Corvoni (brokered by House Hanassa). Lord Jeramiah Cecilio killed by Antonio Salernus. Duchess B--'s duel that closed the Pitt House Theatre. Councilman Urtain's champion fighting on a sky cruise. Violence as a formal mechanism for settling scores.
